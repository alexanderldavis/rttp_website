A Voté! L'Élection Fraçaise de 2002

Use this website to learn assignment details and due dates, as well as keep up to date on daily game objectives. Reacting to the Past (RTTP) simulation games are designed to build educational communities and engage students in abstract thought outside of the classroom, and this website aims to help students remain on track of their assignments.

RTTP are simulation games based on moments of historical crises. Students are assigned character roles, which provide an outline to the stance they will be promoting throughout the simulation.

This RTTP game simulation concerns the controversial French election of 2002, which ended in a surprise run-off between a conservative candidate and a candidate representing the alt-right. This election reflects a change in European politics, with an emphasis on the role of the media in shaping public opinion: in the months before the election, the campaign had indeed increasingly focused on questions of law and order, with a particular focus on alleged crimes committed by young people, especially those of immigrant origins. Many alarmist reports on TF1 and France 2 (main television channels) emphasized this alleged crime wave and portrayed Prime Minister Jospin as “too soft” on crime. Despite Chirac landslide victory, the 2002 election marks a turning point in French politics with the Front National (Le Pen party) becoming the “third largest” political party and since then being able to contest many elections.

Students embody characters ranging from some of the dozens of available candidates from the first round, as well as media organizations and voters, in order to simulate the events that lead up to such a divisive election decision. Students must interact with each other and grapple abstract concepts in a foreign language, providing them with valuable immersion experience.

NOTE: This website will continue to improve, following a test of the game in the Spring semester 300 level French course. Feedback on the use of the website by students in the game will inform any changes to the layout or available information. In later versions of this portal, I hope to implement a sign-in feature to allow students to sign into a personalized website for their character.
