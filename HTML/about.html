About Reacting to the Past


© Luther College Photo Bureau

Reacting to the Past simulations (RTTP) are interactive and unique learning experiences that allow students to immerse themselves into historical events. RTTP encourages student involvement and provides students a venue to formulate abstract ideas in foreign languages. Since RTTP addresses real, historical moments of crisis, students can research these events on their own to increase their chance of winning the simulation. RTTP games also develops friendships and communities of students that continue to interact with each other outside of the classroom.

I like the RTTP games because it brings a fun, different perspective into the classroom. I feel that I learn more and improve my language more while doing the games, than in a regular class.
-Kyla Grau, Luther College '17

While it delivers similar information, RTTP simultaneously fosters the development of community, creativity, and collaborative competition to allow students to understand material by interacting with the material. Empowering students to make decisions in RTTP games enables them to engage with other cultures and develop empathy for historical characters.

Several studies conducted on the use of RTTP curriculum suggests that RTTP can in fact contribute to student communities. Working in conjunction with peers on a consistent basis allow students to form friendships at a faster rate than in ordinary classrooms. These friendships are of particular importance at liberal arts institutions, as students focused on their majors have very few opportunities to interface with students of other majors. By developing these cross-major relationships in language and other courses, students can benefit from a diverse wealth of knowledge and perspectives.

Being in an RTTP game was extremely helpful for me, because it forced me to interact with people in a more conversational way rather than preparing a script for the day or memorizing vocabulary to use. It was definitely competitive, because meeting objectives was fun, and it also helped to create a community in the classroom. I had never met the people in my group before, and after the game, I could consider them to be friends.
-Desiree Bradshaw, Luther College '17

RTTP is an efficient method of building classroom communities with a main goal in mind. Students participating in RTTP games report that they thoroughly enjoyed being able work with fellow peers on a common project, and found that they were more active in their reading of assigned materials.

While it delivers similar information, RTTP simultaneously fosters the development of community, creativity, and collaborative competition to allow students to understand material by interacting with the material. Empowering students to make decisions in RTTP games enables them to engage with other cultures and develop empathy for historical characters.

RTTP is a pedagogy that has been implemented at over 200 Universities and Colleges across the United States. Luther College is proud to host a French program dedicated to implementing the RTTP pedagogy in its classrooms.
